PhD Opportunities

We are seeking highly capable and self-motivated PhD candidates to be enrolled in the Priority Research Centre for Geotechnical Science and Engineering (PRCGSE) at the University of Newcastle (UoN), Australia, ranked in the top 175 in the world, according to the 2024 QS World University Rankings. The candidates are expected to apply China Scholarship Council (CSC) in Mar. 2025 and enrol in Sep. 2025

Research topic: Numerical modelling of large-scale problems in geotechnical engineering

In 2012, Prof JingSong Huang and his colleagues developed a novel contact dynamics approach based on variational principle. This approach uses convex optimisation to solve governing equations and is very robust. It uses implicit time integration and is also efficient. Prof. JingJing Meng further advanced the approach for modelling rock failure problems and coupled it with the Finite Element Method (FEM) to handle interactions between continuous and discontinuous materials. The PhD candidate will have the opportunity to work with both professors to develop it to consider factors of pore water, temperature and, particle breakage, nonlinear contact laws, etc., or to apply it in studying various geohazards under extreme climates. The candidate can also develop it towards digital twins of large infrastructures projects for their risk assessment or economical maintenance aided by machine learning.
